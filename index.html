DID YOU KNOW THAT

The term martial arts refers to all of the various systems of training for combat that have been arranged or systematized. Generally, these different systems are all designed for one purpose: physically defeating opponents and defending against threats. The art of combat, wrestling particulary, has been part of mankind since its beginnings.Martial arts evolved with the development of the human physiology and has grown into what we now recognise as martial arts in the modern era.







TOP 10 MARTIAL ARTS

1



BOXING

Boxing is a modern form of historical one-on-one hand combat that is probably as old as humans themselves.

2



JIU-JITSU

Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) is a self-defense martial art and combat sport based on grappling, ground fighting and submission holds.

3



JUDO

Judo (柔道, jūdō, lit. “gentle way”) is modern martial art, which has since evolved into a combat and Olympic sport.

4



KARATE

One of the most well-known out of all of the martial arts, it features some impressive movements of powerful punches, kicks and blocks, the Japanese word “karate” translates as “empty handed”.

5



KUNG FU

It’s a popular style of martial art that has gained notoriety through film and television, where some famous practitioners include Jackie Chan, Bruce Lee and Jet Li.

6



MUAY THAI

Hailed as the national sport and cultural martial art of Thailand, Muay Thai dates back to the 13th century and was developed as a form of close-combat.

7



AIKIDO

Developed and created in Japan in the early 1900s, participants are taught how to utilise the strength and energy of the attacker against them in combat.

8



SAMBO

Sambo, the martial art of unarmed self-defense, developed in the USSR where it was practiced by elite units

9



BACOM

The other name of Bacom is Vacon. This is a Peruvian martial art. It is one of the deadliest martial arts.

10



KRAV MAGA

Krav Maga is an Israeli martial art widely accepted in the military, police, and similar branches as a defense.

Faq: Which Martial Arts Technique is Better?

There is no definite answer to this question. Taken from the 170 different martial art techniques, each argues to offer the best techniques. Considering that martial arts are mostly used for self-defense practice, there is no specific way how to measure the effectiveness of each of them. There are a few things to take into consideration, such as the effectiveness, the time it takes to be taught and learned, as well as how easily individuals adapt to them. One might argue that Taekwondo is a good practice of martial arts, while it might be true to a point, using only legs to protect yourself might not be so effective at times. Or take Combat Sambo if you ask any Russian they will most certainly rate it 10 out of 10, however it is not the case for the rest of the individuals. In short, there is no accurate answer to this question.
